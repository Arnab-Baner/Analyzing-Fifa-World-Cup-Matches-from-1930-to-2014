Analyzing the results of Fifa World Cup Matches from 1930 to 2014

About the dataset

The dataset consists of 1673 matches of 83 teams spanning from 1930 to 2014. It has 13 variables some of them being the number of goals scored in a match, the number of goals conceded in the match, match timings, stadium etc.

Goal for the analysis

The goal of this project is to look at the performance of the teams throughout the years against different opponents and whether a particular team performed better or worse against an opponent.

Looking at the total number of matches won, lost and drawn against opponents

Here we can see that Brazil has the highest number of wins in all the years put together. If we select Brazil we can see that they won the highest number of matches against Sweden which is almost 7% of the number of matches that they won. Argentina to my surprise has the second highest losses in all the years put together. 13% of the losses for Argentina were against Germany and England each. Italy and England both have the highest number of draws. Italy had drawn most against Mexico, Germany FR and Argentina. England had drawn most against Sweden.

Looking at the wins of the teams in a year

We can see that Germany has the highest number of wins in the year 2014 and Croatia has the highest average goal difference with a single match 4-0 win against Cameroon. If we select Germany we can look at the teams that they won against and how many goals they scored and conceded against individual team.

Looking at the losses of the teams in a year

Here we can see that Brazil has the highest goal difference. This can be attributed to the historic 7-1 loss against Germany. If we look at Spain their average goal difference was affected by the 5-1 loss against Netherlands.

Best matches in history

Here we can look at the top goal scoring matches in each year. In the year 2002, Germany beat Saudi Arabia for 8-0

Looking at the goals scored by teams in different years

Brazil has the highest number of goals scored with 228 goals followed by Argentina and Germany FR with 144 and 140 goals each. Brazil scored the 228 goals in 104 games with an average of about 2 goals per match. We can see that Brazil scored less in the years 1930, 1934 and 1990 with 7, 4 and 6 goals respectively. Brazil scored the highest number of goals against Sweden with 21 goals in 7 games and the goals contributed almost 10% of the total number of goals scored in all years.

Teams reaching Finals, Quarter Finals and Semi Finals

Germany FR and Brazil reached the finals the most number of times. Brazil won the finals the most number of times.

Looking at the contribution of each team to the total number of goals scored

Here we can see that 30% of the teams are scoring 80% of the goals.

Stats about the Stadiums

The number of stadiums is highest for Italy and Japan which is 20. If we look at England we can look at the location of the 8 stadiums and Wembley had the highest number of World Cup Matches in England. The highest number of matches including all countries took place in Estadio Azteca which is in Mexico.

Timings for the different Matches

We can see that for matches like Round of 16, First Round and Quarter Finals most of the matches took place at 5pm in the evening. For more important matches like semi finals and finals they were played at 8 or 9pm.

Hosting Countries and Winners

No team has ever won the World Cup till 2006 unless they have won it before or they were the hosting team. This holds true if we consider the cancelled World Cup of 1942 which was held by Germany and the World Cup of 1950 which was hosted by Brazil and won by Uruguay.
